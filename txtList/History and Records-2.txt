History and Records
Definition: Virtual reality (VR) is a simulated experience that can be similar to or completely different from the real world. 
Applications: 
entertainment (particularly video games),
 education (such as medical or military training) 
and business (such as virtual meetings)
Term "virtual reality" coined by Jaron Lanier, who in the late 1980s designed some of the first business-grade virtual reality hardware under his firm VPL Research
