Intro

Hosted in Beijing 2/4 - 2/20
91 nations
 Beijing became the first city to have hosted both the Summer and Winter Olympics.
The Games featured a record 109 events across 15 disciplines, with big air freestyle skiing and women's monobob making their Olympic debuts as medal events, as well as several new mixed competitions
 A total of 2,871 athletes representing 91 teams competed in the Games, with Haiti and Saudi Arabia making their Winter Olympic debut.

History

first Winter Olympic Games were held in the French Alps in Chamonix, France in 1924
original six sports—divided into 14 different events—included ski jump, bobsled, curling, ice hockey, skiing, and skating
interrupted in 1940 and 1944 by World War II,
have been hosted on three continents by thirteen countries
Norway leads the all-time Olympic Games medal table 

Results

Norway finished at the top of the medal table for the second successive Winter Olympics, winning a total of 37 medals
of which 16 were gold, setting a new record for the largest number of gold medals won at a single Winter Olympics
host nation China finished third with nine gold medals (albeit in eleventh place by total medals), marking their most successful performance in Winter Olympics history.

Concerns/Controversies

Several countries declined to send any diplomats or official representatives to the games.

An estimated 49 million US gallons of water was expected to be used to create snow at the various venues. 
Artificial snow forms a harder piste compared to real snow. It is often favoured by professionals for being fast and "hyper-grippy" but also raises their fear of falling on it
Positive test result for trimetazidine from the Russian Championships in December for Kamila Valieva of the ROC was belatedly released by a Swedish lab, and her participation at the Olympics became subject to a doping investigation.
